Your Closing Steps
1
Contract Goes to Title & Lender
⌄

Now that you're in contract, David will send everything over to Ticor Title right away. Celene Mincer will be your escrow officer and will be your main point of contact at the title company throughout this process.

The buyer's earnest money deposit is due to Ticor Title within 2 business days. Once that's confirmed, the transaction is officially underway.

A few days before closing, Celene will send you an estimated net sheet that shows your final proceeds after payoff, fees, and any prorations. Give it a look and reach out to David if anything looks off.

Ticor Title handles escrow Net sheet comes before closing Bring your ID to sign
2
Providing Access: Inspections & Appraisal
⌄

The buyers have until May 15 to complete their inspections. You'll need to provide reasonable access for the inspector and any specialists they bring in (like a roofer or HVAC tech). You don't need to be there, but the home should be accessible.

Around the same time, the buyer's lender will order an appraisal. An appraiser will also need access to the home. David will coordinate the scheduling and keep you posted on timing for both.

The appraisal is just confirming that the home's value supports the loan amount. No action needed from you, and David will let you know the outcome once it comes in.

Inspection deadline: May 15 Appraisal happens around the same time David coordinates scheduling
3
Repair Request & Negotiations
⌄

After inspections are done, the buyers will send over a repair request. David will go through it with you and help you decide how to respond.

As the seller, you have options: you can agree to make some or all of the repairs, offer a credit instead, or decline. A credit is often simpler for everyone since you won't have to coordinate contractors before closing.

Keep in mind it's one or the other. You can agree to repairs or offer a credit, but not both. David will walk you through what makes the most sense based on what they're asking for.

If the buyers aren't happy with the outcome, they do have the right to cancel and get their earnest money back. David's goal is to help find a resolution that works so the deal stays together.
Repairs or credit, not both Credit is often the easier path David negotiates on your behalf
4
Final Walkthrough
⌄

The buyers will do a final walkthrough of 210 Heather Lane on May 27 or May 28, typically 1 to 2 days before closing. This is their chance to confirm the home is still in the same condition as when they made their offer.

Make sure any agreed-upon repairs are complete before the walkthrough, and that nothing has been damaged or removed that wasn't supposed to be. The home should be broom-clean and in the condition outlined in the contract.

Since you have a rent-back, you'll still have your belongings in the home at this point. That's completely expected. Just make sure the condition is consistent with what the buyers saw during inspections.

May 27 or 28 Home should match inspection condition Agreed repairs should be done
5
Signing & Closing Day
⌄

You'll sign your closing documents at Ticor Title a day or two before closing, or on closing day itself. Celene will reach out to schedule your signing appointment.

All you need to bring is your driver's license or government-issued ID. Celene will have everything else ready for you.

A few days before closing, Celene will send you a final net sheet that shows exactly how much you'll receive after payoff, prorations, and any credits. Review it with David if you have questions.

On closing day, the title company coordinates with the buyer's lender to fund the loan and record the deed with the county. Once recording is confirmed, the transaction is complete and your proceeds will be wired or issued by check.

Recording typically happens between noon and 5pm on a business day. At that point, the buyers get the keys and you officially have your rent-back period underway. You'll have until June 12 to be fully moved out.
Bring your ID Net sheet arrives before closing Recording: 12pm–5pm Move-out by June 12
